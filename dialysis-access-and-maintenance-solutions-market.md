<p><ul><li>Fistula</li><li>Graft</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dialysis access and maintenance solutions encompass various methods to establish vascular access for patients undergoing hemodialysis. The primary types include fistulas, which involve creating a direct connection between an artery and a vein, providing durable access and lower infection risk. Grafts use synthetic tubing to connect vessels when vein quality is insufficient for fistulas. Other methods may include catheters, used for short-term access. Each type varies in longevity, suitability, and maintenance requirements, influencing patient outcomes in renal therapy.</p></p>
