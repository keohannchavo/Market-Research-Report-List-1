<p><ul><li>Metal Orthopaedic Biomaterial</li><li>Non-Metal Orthopaedic Biomaterial</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The orthopedic biomaterials market is divided into two main categories: metal and non-metal orthopedic biomaterials. Metal orthopedic biomaterials primarily include titanium, stainless steel, and cobalt-chromium alloys, known for their strength, durability, and biocompatibility, making them ideal for implants and prosthetics. Non-metal orthopedic biomaterials encompass polymers, ceramics, and composites, which offer lightweight, flexible, and bioactive properties suitable for various applications. Both types are integral to advancing orthopedic treatments and enhancing patient outcomes in surgical procedures.</p></p>
