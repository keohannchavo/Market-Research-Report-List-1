<p><p>Low Energy Electron Diffraction (LEED) is a surface analysis technique used to study the properties of crystalline surfaces. LEED devices operate by directing low-energy electrons onto a surface and analyzing the resulting diffraction patterns to gain insights into the surface structure and lattice parameters at an atomic level. <p><p>The Low Energy Electron Diffraction (LEED) device market is divided into two main types: Ultra-low Energy Electron Diffraction (ULEED) and Conventional Electron Diffraction (CED). ULEED utilizes lower energy electrons for surface analysis, offering higher spatial resolution and sensitivity to atomic structures and electronic states. In contrast, CED employs higher energy electrons, providing broader applicability in various fields such as materials science and solid-state physics. Both types play crucial roles in characterizing surfaces, aiding in research and development across multiple industries.</p></p>
