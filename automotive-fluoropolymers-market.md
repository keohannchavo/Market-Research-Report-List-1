<p><ul><li>Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE)</li><li>Ethylene Tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE)</li><li>Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP)</li><li>Fluoroelastomer (FKM)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive fluoropolymers market encompasses various types, each serving unique applications. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) is known for its high chemical resistance and low friction properties, making it ideal for seals and gaskets. Ethylene Tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE) offers excellent thermal stability and is used in cable insulation. Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP) combines chemical resistance with flexibility, suitable for coatings. Fluoroelastomers (FKM) provide exceptional resistance to heat and oil, used in fuel systems. Other-specific fluoropolymers address niche needs in automotive applications.</p></p>
